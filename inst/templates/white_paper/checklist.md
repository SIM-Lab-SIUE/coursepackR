# White Paper Project Checklist

## Setup & Planning
- [ ] Form your 2-3 person team.
- [ ] Brainstorm 2-3 potential topics.
- [ ] Get your final topic and RQs/RHs approved by your instructor.
- [ ] Create your team's GitHub repository.
- [ ] All members: Clone the repository to your local machines.
- [ ] Set up your RStudio Project and main `white_paper.qmd` file.
- [ ] Set up your team's Zotero group library and link your `references.bib` file.

## Research & Data Collection
- [ ] Conduct your literature review, adding 10-12 sources to Zotero.
- [ ] Write the first draft of your Introduction & Literature Review sections.
- [ ] Design your survey.
- [ ] Ethics Check: Get your survey questions approved by your instructor.
- [ ] Launch your survey and collect data (Goal: 100+ responses).
- [ ] Close the survey and export the data as `survey_data_raw.csv`.
- [ ] Add the raw data file to your `/data` folder in the GitHub repo.

## Analysis & Writing
- [ ] Write the `Methodology` section.
- [ ] Clean your data using an R script (or a code chunk in your `.qmd`).
- [ ] Analyze your data in R to answer your 2 RQs/RHs.
- [ ] Create at least 2 high-quality visualizations in R (`ggplot2`).
- [ ] Write the `Results` section, embedding your R output and visualizations.
- [ ] Write the `Discussion` section, connecting your results back to your lit review.
- [ ] Write the `Conclusion & Recommendations` section.

## Finalization
- [ ] All team members: Read and edit the entire paper for clarity, style, and flow.
- [ ] Check that all 10-12 sources are cited correctly.
- [ ] Check that your R code runs and your visualizations render properly.
- [ ] Render your final `white_paper.pdf` and add it to the `/output` folder.
- [ ] Update your `README.md` with a final abstract/summary.
- [ ] Push all final changes to GitHub.
- [ ] Submit your GitHub repository link.
